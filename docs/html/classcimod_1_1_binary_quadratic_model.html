◆ interaction_matrix()

template<typename IndexType , typename FloatType >
Matrix cimod::BinaryQuadraticModel< IndexType, FloatType >::interaction_matrix ( const std::vector< IndexType > &  indices) const
inline

generate interaction matrix with given list of indices The generated matrix will be the following symmetric matrix:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} \tilde{J}_{0,0} & \tilde{J}_{0,1} & \tilde{J}_{0,2} & \cdots \\ \tilde{J}_{1,0} & \tilde{J}_{1,1} & \tilde{J}_{1,2} & \cdots \\ \tilde{J}_{2,0} & \tilde{J}_{2,1} & \tilde{J}_{2,2} & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots \\ \end{pmatrix} \]

where in the Ising case,

\[ \tilde{J}_{f(i),f(j)} = J_{ij} + J_{ji}, \\ \tilde{J}_{f(i),f(i)} = h_{i}, \]

and the QUBO case,

\[ \tilde{J}_{f(i),f(j)} = Q_{ij} + Q_{ji}, \\ \tilde{J}_{f(i),f(i)} = Q_{ii}, \]

and the function \(f\) denotes a mapping from index to the corresponding number specified by the argument indices. For instance, if indices is ['a', 'b', 'c'], The following equations, \(f(a) = 0, f(b)=1, \mathrm{and} f(c)=2\) hold.

The original Hamiltonian can be rewritten with \(\tilde{J_{ij}}\) as

\[ E_{\mathrm{Ising}} = \sum_{i} \tilde{J}_{f(i),f(i)} s_i + \sum_{i < j} \tilde{J}_{f(i), f(j)} s_i s_j + \delta_{\mathrm{Ising}}, \]

and

\[ E_{\mathrm{QUBO}} = \sum_{i} \tilde{J}_{f(i), f(i)}x_i + \sum_{i < j} \tilde{J}_{f(i), f(j)} x_i x_j + \delta_{\mathrm{QUBO}}. \]
